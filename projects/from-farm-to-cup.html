From Farm to Cup

A bootstrap confidence interval project that studies coffee farmer income uncertainty and explains why a single average is not enough for uneven livelihood data.

Project focus

Full Content

This project presents statistical uncertainty in a way that is easier to explain to non-technical readers. It compares traditional confidence intervals with bootstrap-based intervals and connects the output to farmer support decisions.

Problem

Coffee farmer income can vary widely, so a simple mean may hide risk. The project asks how income uncertainty can be estimated and explained more responsibly.

Data

The project used a synthetic dataset of 200 Bukidnon coffee farmer records. The dataset was used to model uneven income patterns and compare interval estimates.

Method

The workflow used R, bootstrap resampling, percentile intervals, and BCa confidence intervals to estimate farmer income under uncertainty.

Result

Bootstrap outputs made the income range easier to communicate than a single point estimate. The project showed where uncertainty should be included in interpretation.

Impact

The analysis supports better livelihood and policy conversations by showing income estimates with uncertainty, not just an average value.
